Privacy Policy

Last Updated: December 27, 2025

This Privacy Policy explains how the Buck Music Bot ("the Bot") handles data within your Discord server.

1. Data We Collect

We believe in minimal data collection. The Bot only processes the following information to function:

  • User IDs: To identify authorized users for specific commands (e.g., the download command).
  • Guild/Channel IDs: To know where to play music, where to log messages, and where to execute commands.
  • Command Content: To process your requests (e.g., search queries for music).
  • Message Metadata: For the message logging feature (if enabled by server admins), we temporarily process message content to generate logs. We do not store these logs on our own external databases.

2. How We Use Data

Your data is used solely for the following purposes:

  • To facilitate music streaming and downloads from the connected Navidrome server.
  • To provide moderation tools like the `/purge` command.
  • To update the Bot's status with the currently playing track.
  • To provide server activity logs in the designated channel set by your administrators.

3. Data Storage and Security

Most data processed by the Bot is ephemeral and stored in memory only while the Bot is running. Permanent settings (like log channel IDs) are stored in a local `database.json` file. We do not sell or share your data with third parties.

4. Third-Party Services

The Bot interacts with the following third-party services:

  • Discord: To deliver our services to your server.
  • Navidrome: To fetch music and metadata from the private media server.
  • LrcLib: To fetch song lyrics.
  • Spotify API: To fetch metadata for music downloads.

5. Your Rights

Administrators can stop the Bot's data processing at any time by removing the Bot from the server. To request the deletion of any stored configuration data, please contact the Bot owner.

6. Changes to This Policy

We may update this policy occasionally. Continued use of the Bot after changes constitutes acceptance of the new policy.
